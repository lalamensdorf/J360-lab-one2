Biography

Highlights of Hillary Clinton's Life:

When Hillary Clinton was elected to the U.S. Senate in 2001, she became the first woman in the United States to win a public office seat.

BACKGROUND

Hillary Diane Clinton was born Hillary Diane Rodham in 1947 in Chicago, Illinois.

Hillary's father was a fabric store owner, and her mother,Dorothy Rodham, gave birth to two sons after Hillary.

EDUCATION AND EARLY CAREER

Rodham attended Wellesley College, where she was active in student politics. After Wellesley, she attended Yale Law School, where she met her future husband, Bill Clinton. Graduating with honors in 1973, she enrolled at the Yale Child Study Center, where she took courses on children and medicine .

MARRIAGE TO BILL CLINTON

Hillary Rodham married Bill Clinton on October 11, 1975, at their home in Fayetteville, Arkansas. Bill Clinton had purchased a house for Hillary before he proposed as a surprise. Bill and Hillary's daughter, Chelsea, was born on February 27, 1980.

U.S. SECRETARY of STATE

After being elected President of the United States, Barack Obama nominated Hillary Clinton as Secretary of State. She began her term as the 67th U.S. Secretary of State on January 21, 2009.

During her term, Clinton used her position to advocate for women's and human rights.

Under Hillary's leadership the State Department came under investigation. There was a deadly attack on a U.S. diplomatic post in Benghazi, Libya, which killed U.S. ambassador Christopher Stevens and three others on September 11, 2012. An in regards to the Benghazi attack, an independent panel found "systematic failures and leadership and management deficiencies" at the State Department.
